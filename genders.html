Gender Identities

Many people identify with a different gender than they were assigned at birth. This page will go through some of the more common identities and what they are.

This is the transgender flag. Someone who is transgender identifies as a different gender than they were assigned at birth. Some may get surgery to biologically match their true gender, but many dont as well

This is the nonbinary flag. Someone who is nonbinary identifies as someone not male nor female. Many of them use they/them pronouns along with any other pronouns they feel comfortable with.

This is the gender queer flag. Someone who is gender queer is unsure of their gender or don't know what gender they identify with. Some may just not want to identify with a specific label as well.

This is the agender flag. Someone who is agender is someone who doesn't gave a gender or doesnt identify as a specific gender. 

This is the gender fluid flag. Someone who is genderfluid has a gender that may flucuate from day to day. Some times they may feel more feminine, others more masucline, or more nonbinary.

This is the bigender flag. Someone who is bigender is someone who identifies with two different genders.

This is the two-spirit flag. Two Spirit is a term that is for different sexualities and genders in Indigenous Native American tribes.
